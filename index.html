Check days-on-market for the ZIP

Visit at different times of day
High investor ownership often means more rentals and different community dynamics than you'd see in a listing photo.
Healthy signs: Mostly owner-occupied, well-maintained, rising but not spiking prices, days on market 7–20 days.

High-risk signs: Investor ownership above 40%, multiple homes same LLC owner, days on market under 3, recent large portfolio purchases in county records.
3
Making a competitive offer
Go time
Write a personal letter to the seller Investors can't do this
Investors offer cash but not a story. A sincere letter about why you want to live there can tip a close decision in your favor.
Include an escalation clause
Automatically beats competing offers by a set amount up to your max. Example: "I'll beat any offer by $2,000 up to $199,000."
Offer a flexible closing date
Ask what timeline works for the seller before submitting. Costs nothing and often beats a rigid cash offer.
Increase earnest money to 2–3%
On a $180,000 home that's $3,600–$5,400 vs the standard $1,800 — a meaningful signal of serious intent.
Ask your agent for backup position — if the cash deal falls through you may be next. Ohio law requires all offers be presented to the seller, so you always have the right to be heard.
